Stylistic Defaults

Major: (generally I and IV chords in major keys) Swing musicians would always voice these as Major 6th chords. Major 7ths are very un-swing.

Minor: (generally i and iv chords in minor keys) Swing arrangers always voiced these as minor 6th chords. Use a minor 6th voicing unless the minor chord has the function of a minor 7.*

Dominant: Dominant chords cycle backwards. They have a partner in crime, which is the minor 7th chord. Know when to let the dominant chord work solo.

Minor 7th: *Minor 7th chords lead into a dominant chord. Swing harmony uses minor 7th chords for vamps (like I6-vi7-ii7-V7), and cadences. Cut out the extra minor 7ths.

Diminished: Diminshed chords have role as a passing chord.

Augmented: They are basically colored V7 chords.
