Guitar Things

Strings

Martin SP 80/20
.013 gauge sets or higher

Posture

Allan Reuss and Freddie Green have a similar playing position. Both cross their left leg over right, and sit the guitar on their left leg, with the guitar angled back, so that the back of the guitar doesn't touch anything. The neck is angled up a bit, too.

Left Hand Technique

The left hand is the most important part of the equation, responsible for how long fretted notes ring. You'll want to "pump" your fingers for each beat. Your fingers will never completely leave the strings.

Right Hand Technique

Avoid emphasizing the low E string, because it can easy buzz against the fretboard if you hit hard. Emphasize the D and G strings.

Some prefer to just strum away over the end of the fretboard (where a neck pickup would be). Others alternate between the neck pickup area on beats 1 and 3, and the bridge pickup area on beats 2 and 4.
